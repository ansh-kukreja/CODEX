AirChat Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) Prevention Policy

Last updated: 15/06/2025

1. About AirChat

AirChat is a social media platform that allows users to share images and text-based content and engage in private and public chats with other users. While we encourage freedom of expression and open communication, we place the highest priority on user safety, particularly the protection of minors.

2. Zero Tolerance Policy

We maintain a zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse material (CSAM), child exploitation, and any content or activity that promotes or facilitates CSAE. Any user found to be engaging in such behavior will be subject to immediate account suspension or termination, and relevant authorities may be notified.

Prohibited content includes, but is not limited to:

  • Any images, videos, or text depicting or referencing CSAE
  • Content that sexually exploits or endangers minors
  • Grooming, solicitation, or any attempt to exploit minors
  • Links or references to external content involving CSAE

3. Content Monitoring and Moderation

Our moderation system involves a combination of manual review and proactive automated detection:

  • Human Moderation Team: A dedicated team continuously monitors content shared on AirChat, including text posts, images, and chats.
  • Automated Detection Tools: We utilize automated tools and machine learning technologies to proactively flag potentially harmful or abusive content for review.
  • User Reporting: Users can report any content or interaction they believe violates our policies directly within the app. All reports are reviewed promptly by our moderation team.

4. Response and Enforcement

Upon detection or reporting of any CSAE-related content:

  • The content is immediately removed.
  • The involved user account is suspended or permanently banned.
  • Relevant law enforcement agencies may be notified where applicable.
  • We fully comply with legal obligations and actively cooperate with child protection organizations and law enforcement authorities to investigate and prevent CSAE.

5. Staff Training

All AirChat moderation staff undergo regular training to:

  • Recognize signs of CSAE and other harmful behaviors.
  • Understand legal obligations and reporting protocols.
  • Stay updated on the latest CSAE trends and preventive measures.

6. External Standards and Compliance

AirChat aligns its CSAE policies with globally recognized standards and best practices, including but not limited to:

  • National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC)
  • Internet Watch Foundation (IWF)
  • Technology Coalition’s Project Protect

We continually review and update our policies to reflect changes in laws, regulations, and best practices related to online child safety.

8. Commitment to Continuous Improvement

The fight against CSAE is ongoing and evolving. AirChat remains committed to:

  • Investing in advanced technologies to detect and prevent CSAE.
  • Working closely with industry partners, child protection organizations, and law enforcement.
  • Educating our users about online safety and responsible behavior.
